How It Works

Most fact-checkers ask: "Is this article true or false?"
That's the wrong question.

The Article Says:
"Senator Johnson claimed that coffee causes cancer."
Layer 1

Did the journalist report accurately?

Did Senator Johnson actually say this? Is the journalist reporting faithfully?

✓ Accurate Reporting

Yes — Senator Johnson said this at a press conference on March 12.

Layer 2

Is the underlying claim true?

Does coffee actually cause cancer? Is what was said factually correct?

✗ False Claim

No — the scientific consensus does not support this.

The reporting is accurate. The claim is false. You need to know both.

The Analysis Pipeline

01

Article Extraction

We fetch the article from the URL you provide (or read the text you paste). We extract the title, author, source, and full article text.

02

Layer 1 — Reporting Accuracy

Claude AI reads the article and identifies every factual assertion the journalist makes. For each one: is this an accurate representation of what happened or was said?

03

Layer 2 — Claim Truth

We extract the actual claims in the article — the things being reported as fact. Then we verify each one using a consensus of four AI systems: Claude, GPT, DeepSeek, and Cohere.

04

Bias & Manipulation Detection

We analyze political leaning, loaded language, framing choices, and whether manipulation techniques are present.

05

Source & Author Credibility

We evaluate the publication's reputation and the author's credentials and history.

06

Trust Score

All findings are synthesized into a single trust score and a plain-English summary. Weighted across all four dimensions.
